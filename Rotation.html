How to play Frost Mage

Frost Mage operates on a strict priority list rather than with a traditional resource system. That means all of your actions will be used in an order of highest value with each GCD, meaning you need to judge your current state throughout an encounter and do some predictions. There are some key concepts that tend to define the whole rotation:

Understanding Shatter Combos

Understanding Shatter Combos

The term Shatter combo is commonly used to refer to any combination of spells that is used to apply a Frozen effect to activate Shatter and deal a lot of quick damage, especially when multiple spells are cast at once so that they both benefit from a single Frozen effect.

Shatter Combos with Flurry

  • Combo:Frostbolt/ Glacial Spike > Flurry > Ice Lance > Ice Lance
  • This combo revolves around Flurry, which applies two stacks of Winter's Chill each time one of its three bolts hits the target. This combo is performed by spell queuing a Flurry after another spell with a cast time to take advantage of spell travel times to get benefit from more than two stacks of Winter's Chill because the spell cast before Flurry will hit after the first bolt but before the third. This combo applies to freeze-immune enemies like raid bosses.

Shatter Combos with Freeze Effects

Frost Mages have many freeze spells that Root or Stun the target and also count them as being Frozen for spells like Shatter. These spells are all broken by damage, but will be active for a brief minimum duration allowing them to be comboed with damage spells when fighting targets like dungeon trash that can be Rooted or Stunned.

  • Frost Nova
  • Ice Nova
  • Cone of Cold
  • Ring of Frost
  • Freeze - This spell is special as it is cast by your Water Elemental, allowing it to be used regardless of what actions you are taking!

Examples of Shatter Combos with Freeze Spells

  • Glacial Spike > freeze spell
  • Frostbolt > freeze spell > Ice Lance
  • Frostbolt/Glacial Spike > Freeze > Ice Lance

Single Target Rotation

Spellslinger Single Target Opener

  1. Pre-cast Frostbolt at 2 seconds
  2. Flurry to Shatter the previous Frostbolt
  3. Icy Veins off global cooldown and Time Warp (unless another lust class uses it)
  4. Frozen Orb
  5. Comet Storm
  6. Ray of Frost if talented
  7. Ice Lance until 5 Icicles
  8. Glacial Spike at 5 Icicles and Flurry unless Winter's Chill is currently on the target
  9. Proceed to normal priority

Spellslinger Single Target Priority

  1. Flurry if your previous cast was Frostbolt or Glacial Spike. and Winter's Chill is not on the target.
  2. Comet Storm if Icy Veins is not active and Winter's Chill is on the target.
  3. Frozen Orb.
  4. Ray of Frost if your previous cast was Flurry
  5. Glacial Spike if Flurry is available or Winter's Chill is on the target.
  6. Shifting Power if Ray of Frost, Frozen Orb and Icy Veins are on cooldown by atleast 12 seconds.
  7. Ice Lance if Winter's Chill is on the target or Fingers of Frost is active.
  8. Frostbolt.

There are two main builds when playing Spellslinger Frost Mage with different playstyles: Death's Chill and Ray of Frost. When playing with Death's Chill, you are most likely to talent into Cold Front and avoid Comet Storm. This makes the priority slightly different: keep Frozen Orb on cooldown and use Flurry and Frostbolt instead of Ice Lance. The Ray of Frost build, however, follows the same priority list and opener as previously mentioned. Cleave is basically the exact same as single target with the only difference is making the decision to talent Comet Storm no matter what. You can gain inspiration or copy talents from different players on Warcraft logs if you want to.

Frostfire Single Target Opener

  1. Pre-cast Frostfire Bolt at 2 seconds
  2. Flurry to Shatter the previous Frostfire Bolt
  3. Icy Veins and Time Warp (unless another lust class uses it)
  4. Comet Storm
  5. Frozen Orb
  6. Frostfire Bolt
  7. Glacial Spike when you have 5 Icicles
  8. Flurry unless Winter's Chill is currently on the target
  9. Proceed to normal priority

Frostfire Single Target Priority

  1. Comet Storm if Flurry is ready or if Winter's Chill is on the target.
  2. Glacial Spike if Flurry is ready and Comet Storm is on cooldown.
  3. Flurry if both Excess buffs are active
  4. Comet Storm
  5. Ray of Frost if Winter's Chill has one stack.
  6. Frozen Orb.
  7. Shifting Power if Ray of Frost, Comet Storm, Frozen Orb and Icy Veins are on cooldown by atleast 12 seconds.
  8. Frostfire Bolt to consume Frostfire Empowerment if Excess Frost is not at 2 stacks and Excess Fire has no stacks.
  9. Ice Lance if Winter's Chill is on the target or Fingers of Frost is active.
  10. Frostfire Bolt.

Frostfire Frost Mage uses both Ray of Frost and Death's Chill in the current best build. While Frostfire Bolt is your main way to generate Brain Freeze, Ice Lance has a higher priority than Frostfire Bolt. You press Frostfire Bolt when you run out of Fingers of Frost and to follow up with Flurry if you have 1 or 2 Icicles. Even the few people who play Cold Front put higher priority on Ice Lance. The priority is almost the exact same when playing single target in m+ with the only difference being not to talent Ray of Frost.

AoE Rotation

Contrary to last season, The difference between Frostfire and Spellslinger is quite large in AoE...

Spellslinger AoE Priority

  1. Cone of Cold if Comet Storm and Frozen Orb were both recently cast
  2. Freeze if Glacial Spike is in progress and won't benefit from Shatter
  3. Frozen Orb
  4. Blizzard
  5. Flurry for Glacial Spike if it won't benefit from Shatter on one of its targets
  6. Ice Nova if Glacial Spike is in progress and won't benefit from Shatter
  7. Frostfire Bolt if Icy Veins has more than 9 seconds remaining and Death's Chill has fewer than 15 stacks (the goal is to build up Death's Chill stacks before starting the rest of the rotation)
  8. Comet Storm
  9. Glacial Spike if you have some way to make it benefit from Shatter
  10. Shifting Power if Icy Veins is on cooldown
  11. Ice Lance if Winter's Chill is or Fingers of Frost is active
  12. Flurry if Winter's Chill is not active
  13. Frostbolt

When playing Spellslinger in AoE the most important thing is to keep Blizzard and Frozen Orb on cooldown as they are your main source of damage

.

Frostfire AoE Priority

  1. Frostfire Bolt if Icy Veins duration is between 30-9 seconds until you have 12 Death's Chill stacks.
  2. Comet Storm
  3. Cone of Cold if previous cast was Comet Storm
  4. Freeze if previous cast was Comet Storm and the enemies are freezable.
  5. Ice Nova if previous cast was Comet Storm and the enemies are freezable.
  6. Freeze if previous cast was Glacial Spike and the enemies are freezable.
  7. Ice Nova if previous cast was Glacial Spike and the enemies are freezable.
  8. Frozen Orb
  9. Blizzard
  10. Ice Lance if Excess Fire is at 2 stacks Or Excess Frost is not at 2 stacks.
  11. Comet Storm if Cone of Cold is on cooldown or won't come up within 10 seconds.
  12. Glacial Spike if you can Shatter it.
  13. Flurry if Excess Frost and Excess Frost are active and Comet Storm is on cooldown.
  14. Flurry if your main target does not have Winter's Chill active.
  15. Frostfire Bolt to consume Frostfire Empowerment if Excess Frost is not at 2 stacks and Excess Fire has no stacks.
  16. Shifting Power if, Frozen Orb, Comet Storm and Icy Veins are on cooldown.
  17. Ice Lance if you have Fingers of Frost or Winter's Chill is on the target.
  18. Frostfire Bolt if you have no other options.

The main thing to take note of is that even though It looks more complicated compared to last season, a lot of it is intuitive once you get the hang of it. There are not actually that many steps as it looks because most of the time you try to keep everything on cooldown with the priority list telling you which ones are the most important for that. Using Excess Fire and Excess Frost slightly suboptimally is not that big of a deal as long as you try and make sure you dont lose any stacks by either pressing Excess Fire when Excess Frost is at 2 stacks, but losing 1 or 2 stacks is ok.
